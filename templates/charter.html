The PEB Union Charter

Official document · Al-Beruni International Boarding School

The official founding charter of the Politics, Economics and Business Union — a student-led organisation at Al-Beruni International Boarding School dedicated to empowering the next generation of informed, responsible leaders.

Article I — Name & Legal Status

1.1 Name. The official name is the Politics, Economics and Business Union, abbreviated as "PEB". The name reflects the three subject fields where students contribute, bringing together like-minded students from the Social Sciences.

1.2 Legal Status. PEB Union is a student-led, non-profit organisation established by students of Al-Beruni International Boarding School. It operates under the school's guidelines and is governed by this charter.

Article II — Mission & Vision

2.1 Mission. Educate and empower students in politics, economics, and business through peer-supported learning. Organise educational activities, MUN simulations, and maintain a digital platform for podcasts and articles to encourage research and dialogue.

2.2 Vision. Build a well-informed, confident, and socially responsible generation of young leaders who actively engage with political, economic, and business issues on a global scale.

2.3 Core Values.

  • Critical Thinking — Ask questions, think deeply, and use evidence to support ideas.
  • Collaboration — Work together, listen, and respect different opinions.
  • Inclusivity — Everyone is welcome and every voice matters.
  • Leadership — Take responsibility, show initiative, and act with integrity.
  • Global Awareness — Stay informed about world issues and understand diverse cultures and perspectives.

Article III — Activities

3.1 Activities. The Union shall:

  • Produce academic and policy-based content.
  • Organise educational and cultural events.
  • Support students academically and intellectually.
  • Promote international awareness and cooperation.

Article IV — Membership

4.1 Eligibility. Open to students who are curious and passionate about contributing to the Union's development. All members must respect and follow the Core Values outlined in Article II.

4.2 Categories.

  • Core Members — Publications, Events, and Executive Councils.
  • Department Members — MUN and Peer-Support teams.
  • General Participants — Al-Beruni and other school students.

4.3 Duties of Members.

Core & Department Members:

  • Follow PEB Union Rules and Ethics.
  • Actively participate in organisational events.
  • Respect and uphold the Core Values of PEB Union.

General Participants:

  • Actively participate in events and activities.
  • Maintain Academic Integrity.
  • Represent the PEB Union appropriately.
  • Provide feedback and suggestions.

Article V — Organisational Structure

5.1 Structure. The PEB Union is governed by three main councils and two departments, each with distinct roles that together ensure effective management, knowledge production, and student engagement.

  • Executive Council — provides strategic direction and oversight.
  • Core Members — manage publications, research, events, and external relations.
  • Departments — oversee the core operational units and their functions.

5.2 Selection Process.

  1. Nomination — Students self-nominate or are nominated by others, briefly explaining their motivation and contribution.
  2. Open Discussion — A school-wide discussion is organised. Candidates answer questions from students and teachers respectfully.
  3. Decision — Members are selected via transparent vote or a selection committee. Results are announced to the school community.

Equal opportunity applies to all students. No discrimination is permitted at any stage of the process.

Article VI — Publications & Research Council

6.1 Purpose & Mission. Spread awareness on economic, business, and political issues, propose resolutions, and promote the Union through its website and digital publications.

6.2 Team & Responsibilities.

  • Editor — Reviews, edits, and approves all written content produced by the Union.
  • Research Coordinator — Oversees research activities, organises data collection, and ensures accuracy and proper sourcing.
  • Web Developer — Manages and updates the Union's digital platform and uploads publications.

Article VII — Events & External Relations Council

7.1 Purpose & Mission. Organise events and competitions, maintain external relationships with other organisations, and promote discussions related to politics, economics, and business through structured events. Collaborates with all PEB bodies.

7.2 Team. Event Organisers are responsible for organising events and coordinating collaboration with departments and external partners.

7.3 Model United Nations Department.

  • Purpose — Simulate UN bodies to promote international relations, diplomacy, and peaceful resolution of global issues through respectful debates and drafting of resolutions.
  • Mutual Respect — All members must respect each other regardless of role. Organisers freely express opinions, which are discussed collectively among PEB and MUN teams.
  • Communication — Teams must inform participants of events at least 2–3 days in advance and clearly introduce sessions to all participants.
  • External Events — External events must be discussed with the PEB coordinator, Al-Beruni academic director, and MUN members before proceeding.

7.4 Peer-Support Department.

7.4.1 Purpose. Facilitate learning and preparation for assignments in Social Sciences — including Global Politics, Economics, and Business — through extra lessons, peer coordination, and case studies. Aims to improve comprehension, analytical skills, and academic confidence.

7.4.2 Responsibilities. Membership is open to students with strong academic performance and genuine interest. Members are expected to:

  • Actively participate and support peers respectfully.
  • Attend scheduled meetings consistently.
  • Follow the direction of a selected team leader who coordinates activities.

Article VIII — Executive Council

8.1 Role. The Executive Council is the main leadership and decision-making body of PEB Union, ensuring effective management, coordination, and development. It leads in accordance with the Union's values, supports members, promotes leadership, and ensures responsible governance.

8.2 Composition.

  • General Manager — Overall vision, mission, and long-term goals.
  • Finance Manager — Budget, funding, and financial transparency.
  • Council Manager — Coordinates councils and internal communications.
  • Media Manager — Public image and social media platforms.
  • Research Coordinator — Educational resources and academic support.
  • Event Organisers — Logistics, scheduling, and partner coordination.

8.3 Rules & Ethics. Members must act with integrity, responsibility, and professionalism. They shall respect the charter, decisions, and leadership structure. Personal interests must not conflict with Union goals. All actions must reflect fairness, accountability, and respect.

8.4 Disciplinary Measures.

  • Unexcused absences → written warning.
  • Misuse of funds → investigation and possible removal.
  • Abuse of authority → suspension of duties.
  • Repeated failure of responsibilities → reassignment or dismissal.
  • Reputational damage → permanent removal.

Article IX — Financial Management

9.1 Sources of Funding. Funds come exclusively from the school. No external donations or sponsorships unless approved by school administration. All funds must comply with school rules.

9.2 Budgeting. The Finance Manager prepares an annual budget. Councils submit funding requests in advance. Major expenses require approval from the Finance Manager and Executive Council.

9.3 Financial Transparency. Accurate records of all income and expenses are maintained. Monthly reports are presented to the Executive Council and, if required, to school administration.

Article X — Media, Publications & Communications

10.1 Official Platforms.

  • Social media accounts.
  • PEB Union website or school web page.
  • Posters and printed materials.
  • Official communication channels.

Content may only be posted by authorised members under supervision of the Media Manager.

10.2 Content Standards. All content must reflect the mission, values, and objectives of PEB Union. It must be accurate, professional, and respectful. Content must not include personal opinions that misrepresent the Union or share confidential information. All posts should promote learning, leadership, and student engagement.

Article XI — Meetings & Decision-Making

  1. Executive Council Meetings — Held regularly to discuss Union management, finances, and overall direction.
  2. Council Meetings — Organised by individual councils to plan activities and monitor progress.
  3. General Meetings — Held at least once a month for all members to share updates and make collective decisions.

All decisions are made by majority vote. Each member present holds one vote. The Founder holds the deciding vote to maintain transparency. All voting results must be documented in meeting minutes.

Article XII — Amendments to the Charter

12.1 Proposal of Amendments. Any Executive Council or council member may propose amendments in writing, clearly stating the section to be changed and the reason. Amendments may address structure, rules, roles, or procedures, but must align with PEB Union's mission and values.

12.2 Approval Process.

  • Discussed in an Executive Council meeting.
  • Requires a two-thirds majority vote of members present.
  • Communicated to all Union members in writing upon approval.
  • Takes effect immediately unless otherwise specified.

The Founder maintains an updated version of the charter reflecting all amendments.

Summary — PEB Union at a Glance

  • 3 Governing Councils — Executive, Publications & Research, Events & External Relations.
  • 2 Departments — Model United Nations and Peer-Support Team.
  • 5 Core Values — Critical Thinking, Collaboration, Inclusivity, Leadership, Global Awareness.
  • 12 Charter Articles — Covering governance, finance, ethics, media, meetings, and amendments.

Building Tomorrow's Leaders Today — the PEB Union is committed to empowering students through education, collaboration, and global awareness, one discussion, one event, and one resolution at a time.

A student-led union of Al-Beruni International Boarding School, dedicated to educating and empowering the next generation of informed leaders in Politics, Economics and Business.
